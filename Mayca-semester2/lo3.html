Nathalies art

My group and I were tasked with rebranding Nathalie Schilderts art. after doing research, it revealed a diverse audience with a general interest in art and the story behind it. This enabled my group to focus on designing something meaningful for her.

02-Logo

The process behind this was quite simple for me. The client's wish:
- House style: pastel colors, pink and gold
- Logo: blossom incorporated
- Name: international character e.g. 'N-art'.

01-Colors

Recognizing Nathalie's preference for a calm and inviting tone, I took the lead in crafting a color palette that would harmonize with her desired brand ambiance.

03-Typography

Our research revealed that our content will mostly be viewed on mobile screens. This is why the first two fonts are sans-serif fonts and Lora, which adds a touch of sophistication and elegance to the brand identity.

04-Style scape

Creating a style scape was a fun experiment, but due to my limited understanding of rebranding, I constructed the style scape without a clear plan. It was created before we settled on our main colors, typography, and logo, resulting in a disconnect from the rest of the brand.

05-Mock ups

I relied on intuition and visual aesthetics to guide the design process, ensuring that each mock-up captured the essence of our envisioned brand identity.
